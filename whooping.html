The Whooping Crane

The story of the whooping crane is personally important to me and a story I have wanted to tell for a long time. Growing up my Mom was an avid bird watcher and cranes were her favorite bird. I remember dozens of weekends where we would drive up to Necedah or Horicon marsh in the hopes of seeing a whooping crane in the wild. We never saw one. I estimate it was around 10 years of searching and hoping before we finally got lucky. One day we were driving on the interstate and there was a whooping crane in plain sight just next to the road. Their bright white plumage is impossible to miss. To this day it is still the only time I have seen a whooping crane in the wild. Conservation is largely driven by narratives. There are countless species that need help, but we choose to save to animals we know and care about.I hope this story gets you to care about the whooping crane.
